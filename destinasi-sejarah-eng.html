Borobudur Temple

Borobudur Temple is the largest Buddhist temple in the world, located in Magelang, Central Java, Indonesia. Built in the 9th century by the Sailendra dynasty, this temple has stunning architecture with tiered structures and beautiful Buddha reliefs. Borobudur also offers breathtaking views, especially during sunrise or sunset. It is one of the most important historical and spiritual sites in Indonesia, often visited by both local and international tourists.

Kraton Yogyakarta

Yogyakarta Palace, or more formally Kraton Ngayogyakarta Hadiningrat, is the official palace of the Yogyakarta Sultanate located in Yogyakarta City, Special Region of Yogyakarta. The Yogyakarta Palace is located in the center of the Special Region of Yogyakarta. The area of the Yogyakarta Palace is 14,000 square meters. Inside, there are many buildings used as the residence of the sultan and his family, as well as palace servants. To the north, there is the northern square and to the south, there is the southern square, about 10 minutes from the Malioboro area.

Old Town Jakarta

Old Town Jakarta, also known as Batavia Lama (Oud Batavia), is a small area in Jakarta, Indonesia. In this area, there are various well-preserved old buildings, each with its own history. These buildings include the Fatahillah Museum, Bank Indonesia Museum, Red Shop Kota Tua, and many more.

Prambanan Temple

Prambanan Temple is the largest Hindu temple complex in Indonesia, located about 17 kilometers from Yogyakarta, in Central Java. Built in the 9th century AD, this temple is a symbol of the grandeur of classical Javanese Hindu architecture. Its stunning beauty and complexity of reliefs are remarkable, with astonishing architecture and captivating ornamentation details. The temple consists of several main temples, including the tall and elegant Shiva Temple, which is the center of attention. The uniqueness and beauty of Prambanan Temple make it one of the most popular cultural tourist destinations in Indonesia.

Taman Mini Indonesia Indah

Taman Mini Indonesia Indah is a cultural-themed amusement park in East Jakarta, DKI Jakarta, Indonesia, covering an area of ​​approximately 147 hectares or 1.47 square kilometers. This park is a summary of Indonesian culture, which includes various aspects of the daily life of the people of 26 provinces of Indonesia displayed in traditional architectural regional pavilions, as well as showcasing various regional costumes, dances, and traditions. In addition, in the middle of TMII, there is a lake that depicts miniature Indonesian archipelago in its center, which also serves as a venue for theater performances.
